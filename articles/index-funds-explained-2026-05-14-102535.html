What Are Index Funds and Why Do They Beat Stock Pickers?

Investing 2026-05-14 · 5 min read
← All Guides

What Is an Index Fund?

An index fund holds stocks that mirror a market index — most commonly the S&P 500 (500 largest US companies).

Instead of picking individual stocks, you buy tiny pieces of all 500 companies at once. When the market goes up, your fund goes up.

The S&P 500 has returned an average of ~10.5% annually since 1957.

Why They Beat Most Active Investors

The S&P 500 SPIVA report: 91% of large-cap active funds underperformed the S&P 500 over 20 years.

Reasons: fees eat returns, markets are highly efficient, and past performance doesn't predict future results. Index funds don't try to beat the market — they are the market.

The Best Index Funds to Start With

Start simple:
• US exposure: VOO or VTI
• Global exposure: VT, or 60% VTI + 40% VXUS
• One fund forever: VT

Don't overthink it. Pick one, automate monthly purchases, and check prices less often. That's the entire strategy.
