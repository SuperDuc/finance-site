Why Bitcoin Still Matters in 2026

Bitcoin has matured from a speculative curiosity into a legitimate store of value held by institutional investors, public companies, and even governments.

The good news: buying Bitcoin in 2026 is easier than ever. The whole process takes about 10 minutes if you use the right platform.

Step 1: Choose Your Exchange

Your first decision is which exchange to use. For beginners, we recommend either Coinbase (most beginner-friendly, 600+ coins) or Strike (Bitcoin-only, lowest fees).

If fees are your priority, Strike wins — your first $500 of Bitcoin is completely fee-free.

Step 2: Verify Your Account

Exchanges require identity verification (KYC) — it's the same process as opening a bank account:

1. Sign up with your email
2. Provide your name, address, and date of birth
3. Upload a photo of your government ID
4. Wait for approval (usually instant)

Once verified, link your bank account and you're ready to buy.

How Much Should You Buy?

Financial advisors generally suggest keeping crypto to 5–10% of your total investment portfolio.

If you're brand new, start with an amount you're comfortable losing — treat it as the price of financial education. Consistency beats timing every time.
