Compound Interest Explained: The Math That Makes or Breaks Your Wealth

Investing 2026-05-05 · 5 min read
← All Guides

Simple vs Compound Interest

Compound interest means your money earns money on its money — and that money earns money on that money.

The formula: A = P(1 + r)^t

At 7%: your money grows 3.9× in 20 years, 7.6× in 30 years, 14.9× in 40 years. Time is the main variable.

The Rule of 72

Quick mental math: divide 72 by your interest rate to find years to double your money.

• At 6% → doubles every 12 years
• At 7% → doubles every ~10 years
• At 10% → doubles every 7.2 years

S&P 500 historical return (~10%): your money doubles roughly every 7 years.

Why Starting Early Beats Earning More

Time is worth more than money in investing. Starting at 25 vs 35 isn't a 10-year head start — it's potentially hundreds of thousands of dollars at retirement.

Every year you delay is compounding you can never recover. The best time to start is right now.
